# RealGallery Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 18, 2026

Thank you for using RealGallery.

RealGallery is built around a simple idea: your photos are your files, and they stay that way. The app works entirely on your Mac, requires no account, and transmits nothing.

## The Short Version

* RealGallery collects no personal information — none.
* RealGallery makes no network connections.
* Your photos never leave your Mac.
* Ratings and tags you add are stored inside your own photo files, not in any service.

## Information We Collect

RealGallery does not collect, store, or transmit any personal information. The app has no accounts, no sign-in, and no way for the developer to identify you or see your data. The developer operates no servers.

## Network Access

RealGallery makes no network connections of any kind. The only exceptions are links you explicitly choose to open — such as this Privacy Policy or the support email address — which open in your own browser or mail app.

## Access to Your Files and Folders

RealGallery is a Mac app that works with folders you explicitly select using the standard macOS folder picker. The app can only see folders you have chosen; macOS remembers this permission between launches using security-scoped bookmarks so you don't have to re-select your folders every time.

Within the folders you select, RealGallery reads your image files and their embedded metadata (such as EXIF information: capture date, camera model, lens, exposure settings, and image dimensions) in order to display, organize, search, and filter your photos. RealGallery works with image files only; it does not handle videos.

## RealGallery Writes to Your Files — By Design

Unlike a read-only viewer, RealGallery stores the ratings, color labels, and tags you assign **inside your own files**, so they belong to you and travel with your photos:

* For common formats (JPEG, PNG, HEIC, TIFF), this information is embedded in the photo file's industry-standard XMP metadata.
* For RAW formats, it is written to a separate `.xmp` sidecar file next to the photo, and the RAW file itself is never modified.
* Color labels are also mirrored as macOS Finder tags, so they are visible in Finder.

This means using RealGallery's rating and tagging features modifies the metadata of your files (and updates their modification dates). Your image content — the picture itself — is never altered. Because this metadata uses open standards, it remains readable by Finder, Spotlight, and other photo applications, with no lock-in.

File actions you perform in RealGallery (renaming, moving, copying, deleting to the Trash, and backups) likewise operate on your real files, at your direction, on your own disk.

## Data Stored on Your Mac

To stay fast, RealGallery keeps a small amount of data locally on your Mac in its Application Support folder: thumbnail and preview caches, your app settings, your smart albums, and a catalog that caches photo metadata for quick searching. This data is derived from your own photos, never leaves your Mac, and can be safely deleted (RealGallery rebuilds it automatically).

Your files and RealGallery's local data may be included in backups **you** configure, such as Time Machine or iCloud device backups, according to your own settings.

## Analytics and Tracking

RealGallery contains no analytics services, no advertising SDKs, and no tracking technologies. The app does not track your activity anywhere.

If you installed RealGallery through the Mac App Store, Apple may collect crash reports and usage statistics according to your own macOS privacy settings and Apple's privacy policy. This happens outside the developer's control and involves no photo data.

## Children's Privacy

RealGallery does not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children — it has no mechanism to collect personal information at all.

## Your Rights

Under data protection laws such as the GDPR, you have rights regarding personal data a company holds about you — including access, correction, and deletion. Because RealGallery collects no personal data, there is no personal data for the developer to disclose, correct, or delete. All of your data is on your own Mac, under your control.
